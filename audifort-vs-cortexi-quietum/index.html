Where They Genuinely Differ

Delivery. Audifort and Cortexi are sublingual drops; Quietum Plus is a capsule. Drops held under the tongue absorb partly through the oral tissue, which is the argument both drop-based formulas make for their format. If you dislike holding liquid under your tongue twice a day, the capsule wins on convenience.

Formula philosophy. Audifort casts the widest net — 20+ botanicals spanning circulation, antioxidants, and neural calm (GABA is the interesting inclusion). Cortexi concentrates on a narrower antioxidant core with a cognitive-clarity angle. Quietum Plus takes a traditional herbal-tonic route aimed squarely at tinnitus.

The safety net. This is the most concrete, verifiable difference: Audifort's refund window is 90 days versus 60 for the other two. For products where results take 4–8 weeks to judge, an extra month of refund coverage is a practical advantage, not a marketing one.

What they share: none of these is a medical treatment. No supplement reverses hearing loss or cures tinnitus — all three are support formulas for mild, age-related concerns. If you have significant hearing loss, see an audiologist first.

Comparison FAQ

Is Audifort better than Cortexi?

They share the drop format and similar core logic. Audifort's edge is a broader ingredient list (including GABA) and a 90-day guarantee versus Cortexi's 60. Cortexi's edge is typically price. "Better" depends on whether formula breadth or budget matters more to you.

Is Audifort better than Quietum Plus?

The biggest differences are format (drops vs capsules) and guarantee (90 vs 60 days). Quietum Plus leans on a hawthorn/garlic/vitamin blend; Audifort on a wider botanical mix. Drop-format fans and refund-cautious buyers tend to prefer Audifort.

Can I take two of them together?

Not recommended — the formulas overlap (grape seed, green tea), so stacking risks doubling similar compounds without added benefit. Pick one, use it consistently, and judge within the refund window.

Are any of them a cure for tinnitus?

No. All three are dietary supplements positioned as support for mild concerns — not treatments. Persistent or worsening tinnitus deserves a medical evaluation.
